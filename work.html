Crafting Contagious Connections - Pedagogies of the Hand

My master's project culminated in the creation of a vibrant knowledge-sharing space rooted in personal experience and the integration of hands, heart, and head in learning. Inspired by my grandmother's tactile teaching methods, Crafting Contagious Connections bridges vernacular, experiential learning with modern abstract education.

The project materialized as a participatory space in the KABK garden, where I facilitated workshops in spinning, natural dyeing, and eco-printing, using primarily local resources. By prioritizing learning through doing, the space fostered intergenerational exchange, continuous feedback, and collaborative refinement.

Grounded in John Dewey's experiential learning theory, my thesis critiques the Western emphasis on detached, theoretical knowledge transfer. Instead, it advocates for a holistic, inclusive, and nature-integrated pedagogy, one that values creativity, sustainability, and deep, tactile understanding, challenging conventional Western educational models.

Rerooting Ecologies - Gardens of Hope

An exploration of ecological care and soil regeneration, integrating traditional knowledge systems with contemporary approaches to community care and sustainability. This project examines how gardens can serve as spaces of healing, learning, and regeneration.

Through hands-on engagement with soil, plants, and natural cycles, Rerooting Ecologies creates pathways for reconnecting with the land and understanding our role in ecological systems.

Beyond Borders Material Culture

An investigation into how material practices transcend geographical and cultural boundaries, creating dialogues between diverse making traditions. This project explores the universal language of craft and how traditional techniques travel, adapt, and transform across cultures.

By examining material culture through a cross-cultural lens, the work challenges notions of cultural ownership and celebrates the fluid, interconnected nature of craft knowledge.

House of Making

A collaborative laboratory where different forms of making coexist, transform, and inspire new possibilities for material and cultural exchange. House of Making serves as both a physical and conceptual space for exploration, experimentation, and collective learning.

This project creates environments where makers from diverse backgrounds can share skills, tools, and knowledge, fostering innovation through collaboration and mutual respect.

Making Paper Making Place

An exploration of papermaking as both a craft practice and a way of understanding place, materiality, and the transformation of natural fibers. This project traces the journey from plant to pulp to paper, revealing the deep connections between material, process, and place.

Through hands-on papermaking, participants discover how local materials can be transformed into surfaces for writing, drawing, and making, creating a tangible link to the landscape.

Soil and Craft Ecology

Investigating the relationship between soil health, craft practices, and ecological thinking through hands-on material engagement. This project positions soil not just as a growing medium, but as a material for making and a teacher of ecological principles.

By working directly with earth-based materials and understanding soil as a living ecosystem, the project bridges traditional earthwork techniques with contemporary ecological awareness.

Grassland Crafted

Working with grassland materials to explore sustainable harvesting, processing, and making practices rooted in ecological awareness. This project celebrates the abundance and versatility of grasses as a renewable material source.

Through weaving, basketry, and other grass-based techniques, the work demonstrates how traditional knowledge can inform contemporary sustainable practices.

Craft Document

Documentation practices that capture the tacit knowledge embedded in craft processes, preserving skills and wisdom for future generations. This project explores how we can honor and transmit the unspoken knowledge that lives in the hands of makers.

Through video, photography, writing, and participatory documentation methods, Craft Document creates archives that respect the complexity and nuance of traditional making practices.

Fiber to Textiles

Tracing the journey from raw fiber to finished textile, exploring each step of transformation through hands-on making and traditional techniques. This project demystifies the textile creation process, revealing the labor, skill, and care involved in every thread.

From harvesting and processing plant fibers to spinning, dyeing, and weaving, the work celebrates the complete textile cycle and the deep knowledge systems it contains.

Ramming Earth Natural Building

Engaging with earth as a building material, exploring rammed earth techniques and their potential for sustainable, place-based architecture. This project investigates ancient building methods through a contemporary lens, demonstrating the viability of earth construction today.

Through hands-on building workshops and material experimentation, participants learn how soil composition, compaction techniques, and traditional wisdom combine to create durable, beautiful structures.
