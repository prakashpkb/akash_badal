Crafting Contagious Connections – Pedagogies of Hands

Crafting Contagious Connections grew out of my desire to create a space where learning feels alive — where the hands, heart, and head come together. Inspired by my grandmother's tactile ways of teaching, the project bridges vernacular, experiential learning with more abstract forms of modern education.

The project took shape in the KABK garden as a participatory space for workshops in spinning, natural dyeing, and eco-printing, using mostly local materials. The structure itself was built entirely from found and repurposed materials — old fabrics, reclaimed wood, and traditional wooden joints without a single metal screw. This choice reflected the project's ethos of care, circularity, and connection to the rhythms of craftsmanship.

It became a living, breathing space of intergenerational exchange and shared curiosity, where learning happens through doing, reflecting, and doing again. Rooted in John Dewey's ideas of experiential learning, Crafting Contagious Connections questions the Western tendency toward detached, theoretical education. Instead, it invites a more holistic, inclusive, and nature-integrated way of learning — one that values creativity, regeneration, and deep, tactile understanding.

Rooting Ecologies

Rooting Ecologies is an ongoing action-based research that explores how gardens can become spaces of learning, negotiation, and regeneration in a time of climate disaster. The work draws inspiration from my upbringing in my grandmother's garden and evolved further in the KABK garden in The Hague — a once-neglected plot that has grown into a vibrant ecosystem of plants, pollinators, and community life.

Within such spaces, Rooting Ecologies cultivates encounters between people, soil, and place, transforming the act of gardening into a form of research, conversation, and care. The garden is not simply a site of cultivation, but a space of coexistence and dialogue where questions about the global and the bioregional are worked through in the presence of living systems.

Each seed sown and each weed pulled becomes part of a wider inquiry into how we might inhabit the Earth differently: how we might reconnect with the material, how artists respond to realities that sustain us, and how education itself might take root in the regenerative living world.

Through workshops, conversations, and collaborative planting, participants are invited to engage with the garden as both a pedagogical and ecological commons. Here, learning happens through doing, through touch, observation, weather, and growth. The garden becomes a site where theory and practice meet — where students and communities negotiate the tensions between extraction and regeneration, between global systems and local soils.

In an age defined by environmental collapse, Rooting Ecologies argues that every institution of higher education should nurture a garden — not as an ornamental gesture, but as an essential infrastructure for learning. Gardens bring climate literacy into the body, reconnect knowledge to place and time, and teach patience, collaboration, and reciprocity.

Beyond Borders: Material Culture

Beyond Borders unfolds as a living story — a performative experience where materials speak, move, and remember. It invites us to trace the hidden pathways of the things that surround us: the fibers we wear, the metals we shape, the clays we mold, and the plastics that drift across oceans. Each carries its own rhythm, geography, and memory of touch and transformation.

This work explores how matter travels — mined, grown, traded, assembled, discarded — crossing borders both visible and invisible. Materials shift forms and meanings as they move through hands, lands, and systems of value, revealing our deep entanglement with worlds of production and desire.

Through storytelling, gesture, and sensory engagement, participants are invited to listen to the voices of materials — to sense the distances they have travelled, the care or neglect they have received, and the lives they have shaped. Each object becomes a witness to human intention and ecological consequence.

Beyond Borders is a soft resistance to forgetfulness — a space of reflection that reminds us that every object, every thread, every fragment of matter is part of a vast, interconnected narrative. To encounter material is to encounter the world itself, its histories, its violences, and its tenderness.

House of Making

House of Making is a modular, evolving, and movable public space where creativity, learning, and community come together through simple acts of making. Designed to open and close like a living organism, its structure adapts to the rhythm of people and activities — expanding for collective exchange or folding inward for quiet reflection.

Built from accessible, sustainable materials and guided by hands-on exploration, it invites people to reconnect with creation and with one another. It functions as a living system that gathers intangible traces, gestures, voices, and relationships that emerge through making together.

In this space, process matters more than perfection. Every fold and gesture becomes a conversation between people and their environment, between tradition and innovation. House of Making is a space where making becomes a shared language — one that connects individuals in imagining more sustainable and interconnected futures.

Making Paper, Making Place

Making Paper, Making Place is a hands-on workshop exploring the connections between craft, ecology, and community. Participants forage for papermaking materials — leaves, grasses, seed pods, old notes, and fabric scraps — to create handmade paper that embodies the landscape around them.

Foraging becomes an act of awareness, linking artistic process to ecological cycles and mindful making. Participants learn papermaking and bookbinding techniques while engaging in a shared exploration of place and transformation.

The outcome — a collective sheet of handmade paper — captures the textures, colours, and stories of the day, offering both a personal artefact and a communal record of connection between people, materials, and the natural world.

Grassland Crafted

Working with grassland materials to explore sustainable harvesting, processing, and making practices rooted in ecological awareness. This project celebrates the abundance and versatility of grasses as a renewable material source.

Through weaving, basketry, and other grass-based techniques, the work demonstrates how traditional knowledge can inform contemporary sustainable practices.

Craft Document

Documentation practices that capture the tacit knowledge embedded in craft processes, preserving skills and wisdom for future generations. This project explores how we can honor and transmit the unspoken knowledge that lives in the hands of makers.

Through video, photography, writing, and participatory documentation methods, Craft Document creates archives that respect the complexity and nuance of traditional making practices.

Ramming Earth Natural Building

Engaging with earth as a building material, exploring rammed earth techniques and their potential for sustainable, place-based architecture. This project investigates ancient building methods through a contemporary lens, demonstrating the viability of earth construction today.

Through hands-on building workshops and material experimentation, participants learn how soil composition, compaction techniques, and traditional wisdom combine to create durable, beautiful structures.
